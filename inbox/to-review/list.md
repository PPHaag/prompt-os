01. The Morning Briefing Prompt
Read the context below and generate my morning briefing.

[Paste: overnight emails summary, calendar for today, task list, any urgent items]

Format:
1. URGENT (anything requiring action before 10 AM)
2. TODAY'S PRIORITIES (top 3 things that move the needle)
3. MEETINGS (list with prep notes for each)
4. WAITING ON (items blocked by other people)
5. LOW PRIORITY (can be pushed to tomorrow if needed)

Be direct. No filler. If nothing is urgent, say "Nothing urgent" — do not invent urgency.
I run this every morning. Takes 30 seconds. Replaces 15 minutes of scanning emails and calendars trying to figure out where to start.

1. What was the decision?
2. What options did I consider?

09. The Competitor Analysis Framework
Analyze this competitor: [name, URL, or description]

Evaluate across these dimensions:
1. POSITIONING: What is their core message? Who are they targeting?
2. STRENGTHS: What do they do better than alternatives?
3. WEAKNESSES: Where are they vulnerable?
4. PRICING: How do they charge and is it aligned with value delivered?
5. CONTENT STRATEGY: What topics do they cover and how often?
6. GAPS: What are they NOT doing that they should be?

Conclude with: if I were competing directly with them, what would I do differently?

29. The Reverse Brainstorm
Goal: [what you want to achieve]

First: brainstorm 10 ways to GUARANTEE FAILURE at this goal. Be creative. what would definitely make this fail?

Then: invert each failure into a success strategy.

Then: rank the inverted strategies by which are most counterintuitive but have genuine upside.

Highlight the top 3 with a 2-sentence explanation of each.
This consistently produces better strategy than asking "how do I succeed?" because it forces thinking about failure modes first.
30. The 80/20 Analyzer
Here is everything I am currently working on: [list all projects, tasks, commitments]

Apply the 80/20 principle ruthlessly:
1. Which 20% of these activities are producing 80% of my results?
2. Which activities FEEL productive but are not actually moving me toward my goals?
3. What should I stop doing entirely?
4. What should I double down on?
5. What am I not doing that would have the highest impact if I started?

Be brutally honest. I hired you to tell me the truth, not to make me feel productive.
How to Use This List
Do not try to use all 30 at once. That defeats the purpose.
Start with the Daily Operations section (01-03). Use them for one full week. These save time immediately.
Add Content Creation (04-07) the following week if you create content regularly.
Add your domain-specific section in week three - Research, Coding, or Business depending on your work.
Build the meta-prompts (28-30) into a weekly habit. The Prompt Improver makes every other prompt better. The Weekly Review makes every other week better.
The compounding effect is real. After one month of consistent use, your entire workflow operates at a different level.
